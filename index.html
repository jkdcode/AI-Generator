A haiku is a traditional form of Japanese poetry. The structure typically follows a 5-syllable line, then a 7-syllable line, and finally another 5-syllable line. Haikus often capture a moment in nature, evoke emotion, or highlight the beauty in simplicity. They aim to convey a vivid image or feeling in a concise and contemplative manner.

My life, -
How much more of it remains?
The night is brief.
- Masaoka Shiki
